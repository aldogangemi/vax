  • Converted to RDF-TRiG using the EventCoreference module.
  • Events modeled via GAF-SEM.
  • Perspectives modeled via GRaSP.

The Classic NLP Limitation

Traditional NLP pipelines struggle with extracting knowledge that relies on more than just explicit features.

This results in a "sparsity of connected points," where the deeper, implicit meaning of the text is lost.

Our Central Question

How do we overcome this gap and extract the *implicit knowledge* from the text?

Answering the Question: The Polanyi Method (Theory)

Ontology-Driven Neurosymbolic AI

The "Polanyi" method is our answer. It's an implementation of ontology-driven neurosymbolic context engineering.

It's founded on principles designed to extract the very knowledge that classic NLP misses:

  • Pragmatics: The *intended meaning* and context, not just the literal words.
  • Tacit Knowledge Extraction: Making the *implicit* connections *explicit*.
  • Dynamic Logic: Modeling how situations and beliefs change.

It uses an agentic approach to coordinate multiple components: KG processing, heuristics, and problem-solving tasks.

"Neurosymbolic Graph Enrichment"

The method is described in our paper, "Neurosymbolic graph enrichment for Grounded World Models".

Key capabilities from the research:

  • Combines LLMs with formal knowledge graphs using OWL2.
  • Enriches the graph with implicit knowledge using complex heuristics.
  • Allows for modeling the very frames we need:
    • `semantic implicatures`
    • `moral values`
    • `embodied cognition`
    • `metaphorical representations`

The Polanyi Pipeline in Action (Practice)

Polanyi Demo Interface

The Polanyi tool implements the method as a full pipeline from raw text to an "Extended Knowledge Graph."

The Enrichment Pipeline

  1. Text to AMR: The raw text is parsed into a basic Abstract Meaning Representation (AMR) graph.
  2. AMR to KG: This graph is converted into a formal RDF/OWL Knowledge Graph.
  3. Heuristic Enrichment: The "agentic" system applies heuristics (e.g., `b9-IMPACT`) to infer *implicit* tacit knowledge from the base KG.
  4. Extended KG: The system "back-generates" a final, enriched KG that now includes this new, inferred knowledge.

The Challenge: A Neuro-Cognitive "Frame-Lock"

"COLD" AXIS (Rational / Utilitarian)

Presents arguments based on data, utility, and community benefit. This is a plausible `neuro:dlPFC` (Dorsolateral Prefrontal Cortex) dominant function.

  • Clusters: `Provax`, `Critical`
  • Frames: `mor:PublicGood`, `mor:Utility`, `pbrs:protect-01`
  • Argument: "Here is the data. Let's weigh the options."

"HOT" AXIS (Emotional / Personal)

Hears arguments through a filter of threat, fear, or identity. This is a plausible `neuro:Amygdala` (threat detection) dominant function.

  • Clusters: `Hesitant`, `Antivax`
  • Frames: `emo:Fear`, `mor:Liberty`, `pbrs:lie-01`
  • Argument: "I am scared," "You are forcing me," or "You are lying."

Problem: "Hot" frames are "locked"; they hear "Cold" arguments and re-frame them as threats. The `Amygdala` hijacks the `dlPFC`.

Our 5-Step Methodology

  1. Knowledge extraction (File: vaccinepolanyiextendedgraph.ttl)
    Example: used Polanyi-based AI to create a Knowledge Graph (KG) of a sample `Provax` argument, modeling its intent, strategies, and frames.
  2. Cluster (File: vaccinationontologybasis.txt)
    Analyzed 250-text corpus to identify 5 core attitudinal clusters based on Polanyi heuristics.
  3. Ontology generation (Files: vaxattitude.ttl, vaccinedisputatiokg.ttl)
    Built a formal OWL ontology to define clusters (Part 1) and their conflicts (Part 2).
  4. Map (File: vaccinedisputatiokg-neuro-ext.ttl)
    Mapped each cluster's Active Inference model to plausible neural activations (b20-NEURO) and established psychological profiles.
  5. Apply (Files: vaccinevrscenario.txt, vr-scenario.ttl)
    Designed a VR scenario based *directly* on the formalized knowledge to induce "perspectival thinking."

Step 1: Knowledge Extraction (The "Provax" Example)

Original Text (`polanyi:text_1`)

"In this video, a family physician explains his decision, as a doctor and a parent, to make sure each of his children received HPV vaccine at age 11 or 12. HPV vaccine is cancer prevention. Ask about it for your child."

Base Graph Extraction (B)

AI extracts core frames and entities:

  • fred:physician_1 a fred:FamilyPhysician
  • fred:explain_1 a pbrs:explain-01
  • fred:decide_1 a pbrs:decide-01
  • fred:prevent_1 a pbrs:prevent-01
  • fred:prevent_1 pblr:instrument fred:vaccine_2
  • fred:prevent_1 pblr:theme fred:cancer_1

Heuristic Extension (H)

The AI applies Polanyi heuristics to infer deeper meaning, intent, and argumentation.

  • `b7-COERCION` (Metonymy):
    "vaccine is prevention"
    ...is a polanyi:InstrumentForProcess.
  • `b9-CAUSALITY` (Causality):
    fred:receive_1 caus:causes fred:prevent_1.
  • `b12-PERSPECTIVE` (Perspective):
    polanyi:DoctorPerspective persp:alignsWith polanyi:ParentPerspective.

Step 3 (Part 1): The Attitude Ontology (vaxattitude.ttl)

Core Concepts

The ontology defines the 5 clusters as subclasses of vax:VaccinationAttitude, which is a type of dul:Situation.

Each attitude is *defined by* the collection of frames it uses, connected by the vax:usesFrame property and its sub-properties:

  • vax:ConceptualFrame (e.g., pbrs:protect-01)
  • vax:EmotionalFrame (e.g., emo:Trust)
  • vax:MoralFrame (e.g., mor:PublicGood)
  • vax:MetaphoricalFrame (e.g., blend:VaccineAsShield)

Example: vax:ProvaxAttitude

vax:ProvaxAttitude rdf:type owl:Class ;
  rdfs:subClassOf vax:VaccinationAttitude ,
    [ rdf:type owl:Restriction ;
      owl:onProperty vax:usesConceptualFrame ;
      owl:someValuesFrom pbrs:protect-01
    ] ,
    [ rdf:type owl:Restriction ;
      owl:onProperty vax:usesMoralFrame ;
      owl:someValuesFrom mor:PublicGood
    ] ,
    [ rdf:type owl:Restriction ;
      owl:onProperty vax:usesEmotionalFrame ;
      owl:someValuesFrom emo:Trust
    ] ,
    [ rdf:type owl:Restriction ;
      owl:onProperty vax:usesMetaphoricalFrame ;
      owl:someValuesFrom blend:VaccineAsShield
    ] ;
  rdfs:comment "Stance defined by frames of... 
  protection, public good, and trust."

Explanation: How the Instance "Realizes" the Archetype

The Knowledge Graph on the right is a detailed "ground-truth" example of *how* a text performs the attitude defined by the abstract cluster on the left.

  • mor:PublicGood / emo:Trust
    The cluster's abstract need for Authority/Trust is *realized* by polanyi:Evid_Authority, which points to the fred:decide_1 action being performed by fred:physician_1.
  • pbrs:protect-01
    The cluster's protect-01 frame is *realized* by the specific fred:prevent_1 event, which has its own semantic roles: pblr:instrument fred:vaccine_1 and pblr:theme fred:cancer_1.
  • blend:VaccineAsShield
    The cluster's VaccineAsShield metaphor is *realized* by polanyi:Evid_Fact, which directly equates the fred:vaccine_1 (the tool) with fred:prevent_1 (the action).

Step 5: Application - A VR Scenario

Goal: Not to "convert," but to foster the balanced vax:CriticalAttitude (the dlPFC + TPJ state).

Premise (from vaccinevrscenario.txt):

  • The user plays a Public Health Director for a small town.
  • They must manage two competing meters: vrs:PublicHealthMeter and vrs:PublicTrustMeter.

The Mechanism: Disputatio ad utrumque partem

  1. The user's policy choice (e.g., "Institute Mandate") is their Argument.
  2. The VR avatars (representing the clusters) confront the user with the Embodied Counterargument (from Step 3, Part 2).
  3. The user is forced to experience the consequences of their own "frame-locked" decision.

VR Scenario: The "Hot" vs. "Cold" Zones

1. The "Mayor's Office" (Cold Zone)

Rational, data-driven. User sees dashboards and stats. This zone is designed to engage the `neuro:dlPFC` (from Step 4).

Emotional, personal, conflict. User faces citizens. This zone is designed to engage the `neuro:Amygdala` (from Step 4).

  • Cognitive Axis: vrs:HotCognition
  • Engages Frames: `mor:Care`, `mor:Liberty`, `emo:Fear`
  • Avatars: "Hesitant Parent," "Business Owner" (Libertarian)

From KG to Unity: A Data-Driven Pipeline

The KG is not just a model; it's a "director's script" for the game engine. A ScenarioManager.cs script in Unity can:

  1. Query the KG for the user's chosen branch (e.g., :Branch1_ProvaxPolicy).
  2. Find the required encounters (e.g., :Encounter1_BusinessOwner).
  3. Spawn the correct avatar (vrs:hasAvatar :BusinessOwner).
  4. Query the avatar's attitude (vax:AntivaxLibertarianAttitude) to set its animation state (e.g., "Angry").
  5. Query its argument (kg:Libertarian_Argument) and emotional frame (emo:Anger) to load the correct dialogue and animation trigger.

This creates a flexible, semantically-sound VR experience driven *directly* by the AI analysis.
